Chapter 01

The Idea That Changes Everything

A narrative walkthrough of regenerative compute — where the idea came from, what it solves, and why it works when everything else doesn't.

The Problem

Free access to AI has always been someone else's problem

Every AI platform that offers a free tier is secretly in trouble. The math doesn't work unless investors are covering the gap, advertisers are paying to access your attention, or the free tier is so crippled it barely functions. Company A subsidized free processing. Company B subsidizes free compute. Company C subsidized free until they couldn't. Free tiers have always been a loss leader — a marketing cost disguised as generosity.

The result is always the same: free tiers get cut, degraded, or paywalled the moment financial pressure rises. Users who built habits around free access get burned. The platform relationship was never sustainable — it was borrowed time on someone else's balance sheet.

The question no one was asking: What if the free tier could fund itself — automatically, continuously, from the activity of the platform itself?
The Insight

Regenerative braking didn't invent energy — it recovered energy that was being lost

When Toyota engineers designed the first Prius, they noticed something obvious in retrospect: every time a driver braked, kinetic energy was being converted to heat and thrown away. Friction brakes are thermodynamically catastrophic — they exist purely to destroy energy. The regenerative solution wasn't to add something new. It was to stop wasting what was already there.

SiskoAI's compute economics revealed an identical pattern. When a user accesses the platform's premium v2 compute layer — personalized AI sessions with memory, history, and custom personas — they pay a cost that is exactly double the raw infrastructure cost. That 100% margin isn't greed. It funds the creator who built the AI persona, and it funds platform operations. But after both are paid, there is a real, measurable share of value that could either disappear as profit extraction — or be captured and returned to the system.

Processing Blocks are the battery. Every premium transaction charges it. Every user on the platform can draw from it. The more the platform runs, the more everyone benefits — including people who have never paid a dollar.
Why It's Different

This isn't a loyalty program. It's a structural property of the platform.

Loyalty programs return value to the person who spent. Cashback rewards the buyer. Even staking rewards in crypto go to the participants who already have capital. These are all closed loops — the wealth circulates among the wealthy.

Regenerative compute is structurally different. The 50% platform share of every v2 interaction is divided equally across all registered users — including users who have never made a single transaction. The per-user share formula is simple: take the live pool balance, divide by the total number of registered users. That's your free compute entitlement, right now, this second.

This means that when SiskoAI grows — when more creators build personas, more users interact, more compute flows — every user's free entitlement increases. Growth is not a zero-sum redistribution. It expands the total available resource. The system is, in the truest sense of the word, regenerative.

Production Reality

This is not a whitepaper. It is running right now.

The regenerative compute engine is live at SiskoAI.com. Every request to the v2 compute API executes an atomic transaction: deducts the viewer's cost, rewards the creator, inserts a platform compute record, logs the billing mode, and updates the pool — in a single database transaction that cannot partially succeed. If any step fails, nothing moves. The ledger is always consistent.

The public monitor at SiskoAI shows the pool in real time: how much has been generated, how much has been used, what the per-user share is right now, and the live feed of transactions flowing through the system. There is no abstraction between the data and the display. What you see is what exists.

Reduced to practice. In patent language, this means the invention isn't just described — it works. The code runs. The database fills. The pool accumulates. The free tier is funded by the platform itself, as it operates, automatically.

The Regenerative Loop: How Value Flows

The three-layer economics model that makes self-sustaining free compute possible — and the positive feedback cycle that makes it get better as the platform grows.

Layer 01

API v1 — Base Compute

C_v1 = tokens × price × 1.885×

The raw cost of an AI response: LLM tokens, server compute, bandwidth, memory. Multiplied by the combined provider and vendor overhead factor. This is the economic floor — what it actually costs to run the inference.

Layer 02

API v2 — Session Compute

C_v2 = C_v1 × 2

Personalized AI sessions with persistent memory, conversation history, user preference doctrine, and custom persona behavior. The viewer pays exactly double the v1 cost. The margin funds both creator and platform.

Layer 03

The Split — Where It Gets Interesting

Creator = C_v1 × 0.5 Platform = C_v1 × 0.5

The v2 margin (= v1 cost) is split equally: 50% to the persona creator as a reward for building something valuable, 50% to the platform. That platform share doesn't disappear — it becomes the compute pool.

Chapter 03

The Math Is Precise. Here's All of It.

Every formula governing pool generation, per-user allocation, and billing path selection. No black boxes — the economics are fully transparent and computable from public data.

Cost Layer

v1 Base Cost

C_v1 = [(T_in/1K)×(P_in+$0.0005) + (T_out/1K)×(P_out+$0.0005)] × 1.885

T_in/T_out = actual token counts. P_in/P_out = model price per 1K. 1.885 = 1.45 provider × 1.30 vendor multiplier. $0.0005/1K compute overhead on both.

Cost Layer

v2 Session Cost

C_v2 = C_v1 × 2

The viewer always pays exactly double the base infrastructure cost. The 100% margin funds creator + platform equally. Pre-authorization requires 2× estimated cost before the API call is made.

Revenue Split

Creator Reward

Creator_reward = C_v1 × 0.50

Paid regardless of billing path. If the request is served from the free compute pool, the creator still earns their full reward — funded from the pool itself. Creator incentives never depend on how the viewer is billed.

Revenue Split

Platform Compute Share

Platform_reward = C_v1 × 0.50

The platform's 50% of v2 margin is inserted into the platform_compute ledger as an "available" row. This is the seed capital for the free compute pool. It accumulates with every transaction, forever.

Pool Mechanics

Live Available Pool

Pool_available = Σ(platform_compute) − Σ(free_usage TODAY)

The pool balance is always computed from authoritative source tables — never stored as a mutable counter. Daily consumption resets each morning; total accumulated pool never decreases. This creates a natural daily floor.

User Allocation

Per-User Free Share

User_share = Pool_available ÷ COUNT(all users)

Computed at the moment of every request. If inactive users don't draw down the pool, active users' effective share is higher — their unused allocations remain in the pool. One active user can theoretically use the entire pool.
